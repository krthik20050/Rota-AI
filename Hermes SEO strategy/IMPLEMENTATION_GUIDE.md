## THE HONEST TRUTH ABOUT 30 BLOG POSTS

Here is the problem. I wrote 30 blog posts. They all have the same DNA to some degree. Same voice, same rhythms, same general structure. Even with varied formatting nd personal stories, a careful reader will notice the pattern.

Google is also getting better at detecting AI-origin content. Publishing 30 AI-assisted posts at once will not help your SEO. It might actually hurt it.

Here is what I recommend instead.

## PHASE 1: LAUNCH WEEK (Week 1-2)

### Do NOT publish blog posts yet

Focus on getting the website live nd getting initial traction. The blog comes later.

### Week 1 Tasks

1. **Launch the website** with these pages only:
   - Homepage
   - Download/Install page
   - Comparison page (Rota AI vs Wispr Flow)
   - Blog index page (empty for now)
   - About page (your story, brief)

2. **Set up technical SEO**
   - Meta tags on every page
   - Schema markup (SoftwareApplication on homepage, FAQPage on pages with FAQs)
   - Sitemap.xml
   - Robots.txt
   - llms.txt

3. **Launch on Product Hunt**

4. **Post on Hacker News** (Show HN)

5. **Post on 2 subreddits**: r/opensource nd r/windows

## PHASE 2: CONTENT ENGINE (Week 3-8)

### Publish 2 blog posts per week. Not more.

Quality over quantity. Each post should be useful enough that someone would bookmark it or share it.

### Which posts to publish first (priority order)

These are the posts most likely to get search traffic nd backlinks:

1. **Your origin story** (post 01) — this is your most shareable piece of content
2. **Rota AI vs Wispr Flow** (post 02) — high search volume, direct comparison
3. **How to run offline voice dictation** (post 05) — targets privacy angle
4. **Wispr Flow alternatives free** (post 06) — captures people looking for alternatives
5. **Set up Groq API** (post 09) — tutorial, high intent

That is 5 posts over 3 weeks. After that, let real user questions guide what you write next.

### How to make the posts sound more human before publishing

For each post you publish:

1. **Read it once.** Do not edit yet. Just read nd mark parts that sound "off."

2. **Cut the TL;DR if it just restates the title.** Jump straight into the content.

3. **Cut the first paragraph if it rephrases the title.** AI does this constantly.

4. **Add one personal sentence per section.** Something from your actual experience. "I ran into this when..." or "This took me three hours because..."

5. **Vary the sentence length.** Break some up. Combine others. Make it uneven.

6. **Cut 20% of the content.** AI pads. Remove filler paragraphs.

7. **Read it out loud.** If you stumble, rewrite that part.

8. **Add one mistake or uncertainty.** "I think this is around 90% accurate but I have not measured it carefully." Humans admit uncertainty. AI never does.

## PHASE 3: SUSTAINED GROWTH (Month 3+)

### Keep publishing 1-2 posts per week

By now you should have real users. Read their questions on GitHub Issues, Reddit, Discord. Write posts that answer real questions.

### Build backlinks naturally

- When someone asks "is there a free alternative to Wispr Flow?" on Reddit or a forum, answer genuinely nd link to your comparison page
- When someone asks about offline voice dictation, link to your offline mode page
- Do not spam. Add value first, link second

### Update old posts

Every 2-3 months, go back to your older posts nd update them:
- Fix broken links
- Add new information
- Improve sections that are not performing well
- Update pricing/feature comparisons
